### I. Foundational and Survey Papers on Multi-Agent Systems

1. **"A Survey of Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning with Communication"**  
   Authors: Changxi Zhu, Mehdi Dastani, Shihan Wang  
   Published: 2022-03-16  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2203.08975v2  
   Abstract: Communication is an effective mechanism for coordinating the behaviors of multiple agents in multi-agent deep reinforcement learning. This survey broadly covers techniques, challenges, and progress in this field.

2. **"Multi Agent Systems: An Introduction To Distributed Artificial Intelligence" (PDF)**  
   URL: https://ftp.arcchurches.com/index.jsp/mLAF7A/605407/Multi%20Agent%20Systems%20An%20Introduction%20To%20Distributed%20Artificial%20Intelligence.pdf  
   Description: A foundational textbook/document introducing core principles, architectures, and distributed AI systems formed by multiple agents working collectively.

3. **"What is a Multi-Agent System?" - IBM**  
   URL: https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/multiagent-system  
   Description: IBM's introductory article explaining multi-agent systems, how they work, and their practical applications in industry and AI.

### II. Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) and Cooperation

4. **"Augmenting the action space with conventions to improve multi-agent cooperation in Hanabi"**  
   Authors: F. Bredell, H. A. Engelbrecht, J. C. Schoeman  
   Published: 2024-12-09  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2412.06333v3  
   Abstract: Explores how extending action spaces with conventions improves cooperative behaviors among agents in the partially observable Hanabi game, a benchmark for MARL.

5. **"AOAD-MAT: Transformer-based multi-agent deep reinforcement learning model considering agents' order of action decisions"**  
   Authors: Shota Takayama, Katsuhide Fujita  
   Published: 2025-10-15  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13343v1  
   Abstract: Proposes a transformer-based MARL model that carefully models the order of agents' actions decision-making to improve cooperation in multi-agent environments.

6. **"Deep Reinforcement Learning for Multi-Agent Interaction"**  
   Authors: Ibrahim H. Ahmed, Cillian Brewitt, Ignacio Carlucho et al.  
   Published: 2022-08-02  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2208.01769v1  
   Abstract: Discusses development of autonomous agents capable of interacting in cooperative and competitive scenarios using deep RL approaches.

### III. Game-Theoretic and Decision-Making Models in MAS

7. **"A Hierarchical Game-Theoretic Decision-Making for Cooperative Multi-Agent Systems Under the Presence of Adversarial Agents"**  
   Authors: Qin Yang, Ramviyas Parasuraman  
   Published: 2023-03-28  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2303.16641v1  
   Abstract: Presents a hierarchical game-theoretic model to handle cooperative MAS with adversarial agents, useful in security and defense applications.

8. **"Learning the Value Systems of Agents with Preference-based and Inverse Reinforcement Learning"**  
   Authors: Andrés Holgado-Sánchez, Holger Billhardt, Alberto Fernández et al.  
   Published: 2026-02-04  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04518v1  
   Abstract: Investigates agents' value systems and preferences using inverse reinforcement learning techniques to enable agreement and cooperation.

### IV. System Modeling, Verification, and Simulation of MAS

9. **"A Methodology to Engineer and Validate Dynamic Multi-level Multi-agent Based Simulations"**  
   Authors: Jean-Baptiste Soyez, Gildas Morvan, Daniel Dupont et al.  
   Published: 2013-11-20  
   URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/1311.5108v1  
   Abstract: Proposes a modeling and simulation methodology for complex multi-level MAS, supporting engineering and validation of system behavior.

10. **"Automatic Verification of Parameterised Interleaved Multi-Agent Systems"**  
    Authors: Panagiotis Kouvaros, Alessio Lomuscio  
    Published: 2013-01-28  
    URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/1301.6431v2  
    Abstract: Addresses challenges of model checking verification in MAS where state-space grows exponentially with agent number, introducing scalable approaches.

11. **"From Model-Based Screening to Data-Driven Surrogates: A Multi-Stage Workflow for Exploring Stochastic Agent-Based Models"**  
    Authors: Paul Saves, Matthieu Mastio, Nicolas Verstaevel et al.  
    Published: 2026-04-03  
    URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03350v1  
    Abstract: Presents a workflow integrating design of experiments, surrogate modeling, and analysis for high-dimensional stochastic ABMs, a subclass of MAS.

### V. Control Theory and Stabilization in MAS

12. **"Learning Distributed Stabilizing Controllers for Multi-Agent Systems"**  
    Authors: Gangshan Jing, He Bai, Jemin George et al.  
    Published: 2021-03-07  
    URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2103.04480v1  
    Abstract: Proposes RL-based approaches for distributed stabilization of heterogeneous MAS without explicit models, important for control applications.

13. **"Model-Free Optimal Control of Linear Multi-Agent Systems via Decomposition and Hierarchical Approximation"**  
    Authors: Gangshan Jing, He Bai, Jemin George et al.  
    Published: 2020-08-14  
    URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2008.06604v2  
    Abstract: Addresses efficient optimal control design in large-scale linear MAS using decomposition and hierarchical approaches.

### VII. Explanation and Interpretability in MAS
